Why White Pine?

The Eastern White Pine, also known as the Northern White Pine, once dominated the skyline of what became the Eastern United States including Michigan. Many of these trees in the virgin stands towered as high as 250 feet in height and had trunk diameters in excess of 6 feet. The largest and straightest of these trees were targeted for the masts of sialing ships by the British Royal Navy. Since the colonists were rapidly using up the existing supply of trees close enough to the ocean that were big enough for masts, the kings men were marking trees "reserved" for the English government. Growing resentment to the crown's appropriation of the choicest White Pines helped precipitate the Revolutionary War. The first flag of the revolutionary forces even had a White Pine on its emblem.

The White Pine was naturally selected to dominate the forest skyline, and was a contributing factor in the cause for the fight for freedom.
