Design Principles Assignment

Things that work

  1. Good Affordance Example
    2. Mediamonks.com

    One of the coolest websites I came across. The affordance is really nice on this page the lines are clean and you know exactly what you're clicking on and where the link will take you. It has nice hover effects on almost every element in a way that adds to the design but isn't distracting.

  2. Good Chunking Example
    3. Heavyheavy.com

    This site is simple and clean and chunks the information in a way that is easy to understand and navigate. The user has 6 choices to choose from which makes it a good exampe of hick's law as well. The information is chunked in a logical order that is easy to use and understand.

  3. Good Modularity Example
    4. opnbx.com

    This website does a really good job at showing modularity. The clean design makes it easy to understand whats going on but everything is broken up into seperate sections that the user can interact with. The website scrolls left to right giving different information on different topics after a topic is chosen the user can scroll down and see different information presented in pictures and text that can be expanded and viewed more closely.

  4. Good Signal to Noise Ratio
    5. Union.co

    This website is a great example of good signal to noise ratio in the sense that there is very little noise. The website is clean and ads don't distract you from the content and purpose presented in the website. The interactivity is also really cool when implemented in the design and the colors are simple.

  5. Good Ockham's Razor Example
    6. Andy Shield Portfolio

    Simplicity is best. This website has clean lines, minimal choices to choose from and gets right to the point. The designer has a short, concise "about me" section followed by a short article and some of his work. The page is minimalistic in nature and has a place to contact him. The design is really nice and simple and presents the necessary information in a way that isn't overwhelming and gets right to the point.

Things that don't

  1. Bad Affordance Example
    2. Biology Junction

    With this website there are a lot of distracting elements going on. There are multiple clickable links all labeled under different sections that the user doesn't really know where to go or how to find what they are looking for. On top of that the moving gif's and ad placement is distracting and the page layout is hard to work with.

  2. Bad Chunking Example
    3. BMJ Health Blog

    The link that lead me to this website said their page was "new and improved" which makes me wonder what their old website looked like. The website is pretty text heavy and there isn't really a way to differentiate between the sections. Essentially everything is a clickable link it makes the user not want to stick around to try to find their way around the page.

  3. Bad Modualrity Example
    4. Don Swanson Racing School

    This one is bad for a lot of reasons. The dark background, lack of color scheme and overly vibrant flashy colors make the website distracting. That coupled with the small font, poor design, and zero modularity make the page run on and on with no break between the sections. Instead of using elements to distribute information in a modular fashion throughout the page, the creator simply just changed the color and added line breaks in-between the text sections.

  4. Bad Rule of Thirds Example
    5. Gorwood.com

    This one is bad for a lot of reasons it's essentially just in image with a few links thrown over the top. There are essentially no design elements and the rule of thirds and pretty much every other aspect of good design is nonexistent. Clicking on the links makes things worse when you are brought to other pages with no sense of modularity, chunking, or affordance.

  5. Bad Hick's Law Example
    6. Cool Math

    Hick's law deals with decisions and this website, unfortunately, has many to choose from. Pretty much every icon is a button that links to something else. It makes it difficult to choose what you want to click on with everything being so close together, so colorful, and having a million different options to choose from.
